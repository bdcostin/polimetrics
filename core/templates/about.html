About.

Technology changed the way we live our lives,
so why haven't our political polls changed?



In a world of daily robocalls, constant stimulus, and infrequent trips to the mailbox, traditional avenues of collecting opinion poll data has yielded a declining response rate for the past decade. The result of the 2016 presidential election only raises further questions about the accuracy of opinion polling. Despite a growing body of hard questions, pollsters have not made a concerted effort to adjust their means of collecting and evaluating poll data.

Growing reports from academic circles argue that analysis of social media data is a strong, and in many cases more accurate, predictor of real world outcomes such as the stock market, Brexit, and the 2016 presidential election.

Polmetrics aims to bring these academic insights to the general public by creating an opinion poll that uses the mood sentiment of Tweets.
